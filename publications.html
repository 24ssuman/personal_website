J. Chem. Theory Comput. | 2026 Hydration Free Energies of Alkali Metal and Halide Ions from Data-Driven Many-Body Potentials Suman Saha*, Francesco Paesani*

Summary: This work evaluates hydration free energies of alkali-metal and halide ions in MB-pol water using staged alchemical transformations and data-driven many-body ion potentials. The calculations test ion-water interactions, electrostatics, polarization, and nuclear quantum effects against experimental trends.

ACS Nano | 2026 Carbon Polarization and Carbon Nanotube Stacking Impacts the Behavior of Water in Nanoconfinement Leonard Dick, Richa Rashmi, Henry Agnew, Suman Saha, Xuanyu Zhu, Barbara Kirchner*, Francesco Paesani*

Summary: The paper examines how carbon polarization and nanotube arrangement alter the structure and behavior of water under nanoscale confinement, connecting molecular-level water response to the properties of the confining material.

J. Phys. Chem. Lett. | 2025 Reactivity of Nanoconfined Water Is Modulated by the Properties of Confining Materials Saswata Dasgupta*, Suman Saha, Francesco Paesani*

Summary: This study shows that the chemical and physical properties of confining materials can tune the reactivity of water at nanometer length scales, with implications for interfacial acid-base chemistry and proton-transfer pathways.

J. Am. Chem. Soc. | 2025 Sub-nanometer Confinement Suppresses Autoionization of Water Saswata Dasgupta*, Suman Saha, Francesco Paesani*

Summary: The paper investigates how extreme confinement changes water dissociation, showing that pore dimensions and interfacial environment can strongly suppress autoionization relative to bulk water behavior.
